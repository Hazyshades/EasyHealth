# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

People organizing their own health information, including non-technical users who may feel anxious while reviewing laboratory results. They want to understand what their documents say, keep track of extracted biomarkers, and generate educational summaries without becoming medical experts themselves.

Primary audience: end users managing their own health records.
Secondary audience (later): family members, caregivers, or clinicians reviewing a shared record.

## Product Purpose

EasyHealth turns uploaded medical documents into a reviewable, structured personal health record. It extracts labs and observations, preserves source evidence and provenance, and helps users make sense of the data through charts, reports, and plain-language summaries.

Success looks like: a user uploads a lab report, quickly sees the extracted values, can verify each value against the original page, tracks changes over time, and feels in control of their health information.

## Positioning

The calm, trustworthy health companion that turns scattered medical documents into a clear, reviewable record — without alarm or forced clinical expertise.

## Brand Personality

Calm, trustworthy, concise. The interface stays out of the way, puts ordinary user actions first, and reserves technical details for progressive disclosure. Voice is plain, precise, and reassuring.

## Anti-references

- Developer-console-like primary UI.
- Alarmist medical language.
- Forced technical decisions when the source document does not contain enough evidence.

## Design Principles

- Calm density: show enough to act, hide the rest until it is needed.
- Trust through provenance: always keep source evidence linked to extracted data.
- Plain language first; technical terms appear with context or progressive disclosure.
- Accessible by default: no color-only status, keyboard navigable controls, reduced-motion support.
- Never diagnose: extraction certainty is separate from clinical significance.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG 2.1 AA.
- Support keyboard navigation and screen readers; interactive controls carry visible focus indicators.
- Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` for animations.
- Avoid relying solely on color to communicate status or results.
